Pinterest Case Study
2025



Pinterest Value Proposition

  • Discover/ search engine
  • Visual inspiration
  • Connect to a broader audience
  • Save, group, and organize ideas

    Key Features


Haptic Feedback

Haptic feedback on app when pinning images, making the experience more immersive and responsive


Animation

Animations throughout the app (loading, pinning, clicking images), enhancing user interaction visually and creating a smooth discovery experience


Not Boring

Organized + consistent

Repetitive flow and behavior on every page making Pinterest easily understandable for new users

Why Is Pinterest Better Than Competitors?

1. Curated experience

Content shown on each user’s account isn’t based on what’s popular, but it caters to the user’s interest and what they interact (pin and share) with the most.


2. Continuous


Unlike other competitors, like Instagram or TikTok, Pinterest uses the masonry grid style that does not break the grid abruptly. This pushes users to scroll further down and discover more images subconsiously. The curated Pinterest algorithm also keeps users engaged all the time, unlike the more random feed on Instagram or TikTok.


3. Not monotonic

Throughout Pinterest, there are motion cues and interaction animations that evokes a playful and light vibe, making the whole experience less overwhelming.


4. Straight forward

Pinterest has one main and clear purpose, to display images for users to organize into boards. It focuses on the one simple interaction, omitting side features. Therefore, its easy for people of all ages to get accustomed and understand the flow of Pinterest.


5. Not like other social medias

Pinterest is not about followers, likes, and user to user interactions. More about personal discovery, organizing ideas, collaboration, and visual brainstorming. Making it a stress-free social media environment.


Pain Points

1. Video Advertisements

Random video advertisements throughout the feed that breaks the flow of exploring


2. Narrow/ Restricted Discovery

Once Pinterest understands what the users like, repetitive or similar contents will continue to show up. Although this might help some users create a specific and in-depth board regarding a topic, this could also create a bubble that restricts users from exploring out.


3. Limited Collaboration Tool

Though Pinterest allows users to collaborate on a board, additional tools for collaborations like notes or stickers to comment or vote are not available. Making users need to go back and forth with another product when collaborating.

Solution

1. Video Advertisements


  • Limit advertisements to picture based ads, to appear more cohesive with the image-based feed.
  • Display ads less frequently
  • Restrict small grids for advertisements to minimize clutter
  • Make ads blend-in to the feed rather than putting it on a spotlight
  • Show ads based on user’s interest/ related searches to not disrupt the exploration as well as to achieve more interaction.
